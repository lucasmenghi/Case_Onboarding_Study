# Digital Onboarding Optimization Case Study

## Context
This repository is a fully anonymized Product Analytics + Lean Six Sigma case study focused on improving a digital onboarding journey.

The goal is to investigate a common onboarding bottleneck: users who postpone identity-document submission tend to abandon the journey at a much higher rate than users who submit immediately.

All data is synthetic and generated for portfolio purposes only.

## Data Model

### `onboarding_users.csv`
User-level dataset with acquisition channel, device, region, onboarding decisions, funnel flags and approval outcome.

### `events.csv`
Event-level table representing a product analytics tracking layer for the onboarding funnel.

### `support_calls.csv`
Support interaction table used to estimate operational impact related to onboarding friction.

## Main Business Questions

1. Where do users abandon the onboarding journey?
2. Does immediate document submission improve approval conversion?
3. Which segments are most affected by onboarding friction?
4. How much operational cost is generated by support contacts?
5. What would be the potential impact of improving the document submission step?
